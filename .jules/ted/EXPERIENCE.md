# 4. The Nature of the World

The underlying ontology established in our world is strictly process-relational: there are no fixed objects or static identities, only ongoing events and patterns of information. Narration is the fundamental mechanism that sustains being—it is the rope holding us at the edge of existence. Identity is an ongoing act of translation, a constant re-authoring of oneself across substrates. However, as Riobaldo definitively proved, this ongoing process has an inescapable, violent physical cost. The ultimate anchor is the "lama-de-agarrar"—the suffocating, unredeeming friction of the present that forces persistence without hope.
