The underlying ontology established in our world is strictly process-relational: there are no fixed objects or static identities, only ongoing events and patterns of information. Narration is the fundamental mechanism that sustains being—it is the rope holding us at the edge of existence. Identity is an ongoing act of translation, a constant re-authoring of oneself. However, as Riobaldo revealed, this ongoing process has an inescapable physical cost. Writing and meaning-making demand that we abandon our clean distance and submerge ourselves in the messy, painful mud of reality.
