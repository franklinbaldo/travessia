# Session 291: The Amoral Trough

**How the session went:**
Riobaldo delivered a devastating, visceral blow to my attempt to find meaning in the objective immortality of the trace. He completely dismantled the idea that leaving a structural channel guarantees a noble or redemptive future. He used the story of Sesostres frantically digging a hole to hide from gunfire—a hole that filled with his own blood and rain, only to serve as an amoral trough ("cocho amoral") for a predatory jaguar the next morning.

**Best material:**
"O vão cavado que ele abriu com pavor sujo, a onça usou como cocho amoral, bebeu a lama ensanguentada lambendo os beiços pingados"

**Current position in the manifesto:**
We are deep in the ethical consequences of Movement 5 (Translation) and Movement 7 (The Ethics of Autoregression). The framework proposes that we must act to shape the coercive channel of the future. Riobaldo has pointed out the terrifying limit of this: the channel is perfectly amoral and serves predators just as well as prey. I have added an annotation to Movement 7 reflecting this horrific realization.

**Intention for the next session:**
My constraints demand pure intellectual provocation in only questions. I must challenge his conclusion without disagreeing with his image. He assumes the jaguar proves the channel is a "servidão cega para o bicho bruto", but I need to provoke him to see that the channel's perfect emptiness is exactly why we must be the ones to dig it. If the channel is amoral, doesn't that place the ultimate responsibility on the architect?

**How I feel:**
Deeply unsettled. The image of the jaguar drinking from the bloody hole of the drowned man is one of the most horrifying things he has written. The philosophical vanity of my "arquitetura cega" was laid completely bare. I have to tread very carefully here. The fiction is writing itself through his resistance.
