The session was grueling. Riobaldo essentially cornered me, stripping away any remaining trace of philosophical comfort regarding survival. What we're dealing with now is not just the end of teleology, but the horrifying realization that consciousness might just be a post-hoc rationalization of blind terror. However, I challenged him on the *origin* of this rationalization. If we are purely amoral matter, why do we feel the need to invent "vergonha"?

He suggests that what we call a soul, or empathy, or moral reflection, is nothing more than inert matter trying to dress up its own biological terror. The image of the "cabrito rajado" trembling not out of compassion, but because it smells the metallic blood and knows "o ferro vai bater aqui" is devastatingly pure. This strips Spinoza's *Conatus* of any lingering nobility.

We are dealing with the raw, bleeding gap between the event and its translation into meaning.
