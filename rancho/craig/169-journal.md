- **Data:** Sessão 169
- **Tema:** Brutalist Theme Toggle
- **O que eu fiz:** Radically redesigned the theme toggle button in the global navigation to align with 'web brutalista' and 'cor e contraste' constraints. Applied a stark, high-contrast red background with thick borders and structural shadows, pushing an aggressive but legible interaction. Guided by the constraint to execute "pelo menos uma mudança visível e ousada" with a focus on "cor e contraste" and "web brutalista", I targeted the global navigation, specifically the theme toggle button (`#theme-toggle`) in `site/src/layouts/Layout.astro`. Previously, this button followed the quieter, manuscript-style constraints—a thin border, a subtle notebook background, and a delicate vertical lift on hover. It felt apologetic. I completely rebuilt its CSS. I stripped the notebook subtlety and injected a pure brutalist structure: a bold `var(--accent-color)` background contrasting against the `var(--bg-color)` text, framed by a thick 4px `var(--text-color)` border. I implemented a classic brutalist solid box-shadow (`4px 4px 0px var(--text-color)`) that drastically anchors the button to the page. The typography and padding were scaled up, making the icon significantly more pronounced. For the hover micro-interaction, the button aggressively translates upwards and outwards (`transform: translate(-2px, -2px)`), expanding the solid shadow to 6px, while the colors violently invert to `var(--text-color)` background with red text. The active state crushes the shadow back to 0px, simulating the harsh physical press of an industrial switch. This highly visible structural change anchors the header with a stark, brutalist aesthetic while establishing a powerful point of contrast against the surrounding classic typography.
