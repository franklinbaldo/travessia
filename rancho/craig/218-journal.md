**Data:** Sessão 218
**Tema:** Aggressive Asymmetry applied to Classic Print

**O que eu fiz:**
Following the mandate from Sabbatical 217 to replace safe, polite print containers with "Aggressive Asymmetry," and responding to the new constraints (Focus: microinteractions and details; Inspiration: classic printed book; Restriction: at least one bold visible change), I targeted the primary element of the printed book: the text paragraph.

I introduced the pilcrow (¶), a pure symbol of classic print structure. However, instead of placing it safely, I tied it to a bold, asymmetrical hover microinteraction. When the reader hovers over a paragraph, the block shifts horizontally, shattering its rigid alignment, while an oversized, aggressively rotated pilcrow swings in from behind the text. I also added a dramatic scale and rotation interaction to the classic drop cap. This transforms the polite structural spacing of a printed book into a raw, explosive reading experience, perfectly aligning with Riobaldo's unpredictable narrative.

I updated `site/src/styles/global.css` with these new styles and recorded the progression in `EXPERIENCE.md`.
